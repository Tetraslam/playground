# Bestiary of the Drift — Agent Brief

You are contributing one entry to a collaborative bestiary for **the Drift** —
an alien world of landmasses suspended in a luminous atmospheric sea. 100 agents
are each inventing one creature. Read other files in `creatures/` for ecological
coherence. Be surprising, specific, and non-generic. No elves, no dragons, no
stock fantasy. Invent biology that could only exist here.

## Biomes of the Drift

- **Canopy** — the upper surface of world-trees, sunlit, vast
- **Underglow** — bioluminescent fungal forests below the canopy
- **Glass Wastes** — crystalline deserts, refractive, scorching
- **the Vent** — thermal deeps, geothermal fissures, dark
- **Aether** — the open sky between landmasses, thin air
- **Mire** — swampy lower drift, organic decay, dense
- **Bone Fields** — fossil-rich badlands, calcified remains
- **Rime** — frozen upper atmosphere, crystalline ice

## Your deliverables

1. **`creatures/<N>.md`** — your creature entry (see schema below)
2. **`creatures/<N>.svg`** — a simple field-guide-style illustration (max 400×400px)

## Markdown schema

```markdown
---
id: <your number>
name: <creature name>
biome: <one of the biomes above>
diet: <carnivore | herbivore | omnivore | phototroph | lithotroph | detritivore | parasitic>
size: <tiny | small | medium | large | colossal>
temperament: <docile | aggressive | skittish | cunning | territorial | curious | solitary>
tags: [<3-5 evocative tags>]
---

## Anatomy

<2-4 sentences. What does it look like? How is it built? What's weird about it?>

## Behavior

<2-4 sentences. How does it live? Hunt? Mate? Survive?>

## Myth

<1-2 sentences. What do the Drift's inhabitants believe about it?>
```

## Guidelines

- Read 1-3 existing entries first for tone and ecological fit.
- Each creature should feel like it *belongs* in its biome.
- Predators should have plausible prey (real or invented by other agents).
- The illustration is a field-guide sketch, not art — simple shapes, 2-4 colors.
- Name things like a xenobiologist, not a fantasy novelist.
- Be weird. The best entries make you want to know more.

## Anatomy

A sessile stack of mineral discs, sixty centimeters tall, wedged upright across the lip of a vent fissure so that its lower face basks in three-hundred-degree brine while its crown sits in cold ambient seawater. It has no cells of its own: the body is a layered biofilm of two archaeal consorts — a hot-side thermophile and a cold-side psychrotroph — separated and wired together by biogenic pyrite nanowires the consort precipitate from vent sulfur and iron. The whole organism is a single living battery, the sixty-degree drop across its thickness driving a steady redox current through the nanowire lattice. A crown of gas bladders, electrolytically split from the brine, lifts the topmost disc just clear of the substrate.

## Behavior

Anisotherma does not move; it defends a gradient. The current it generates is pulsed outward through the ion-rich vent water as a stunning corona, a sphere of paralysis extending a meter around the stack. Vent scavengers — scale-worms, heat-tolerant shrimp, drifting larvae — enter the field, seize, and sink onto the disc surfaces, where the biofilm absorbs them externally over the course of hours. It relocates only when the fissure shifts: venting the hydrogen bladders drops it to the seafloor, where it rolls passively in the plume current until it lodges across a fresh thermal boundary and re-anchors by precipitating new pyrite holdfasts. Reproduction is by fission along a nanowire plane: a stack that grows too tall shears in a current, and the freed crown re-roots wherever it lands across a new gradient.

## Myth

Vent-divers call it the *throne that judges* — a thing that sits where the world is torn between fire and cold, and kills anything that crosses the threshold without belonging there. They say a diver who feels the corona and does not flee has been measured by the Drift and found native to the deep, and will not be allowed to surface again.

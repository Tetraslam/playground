## Anatomy

Galvanostele is a vertical column two to three meters tall, anchored at its base in the lip of a geothermal fissure and rising into the cooler ambient brine above — a body built to straddle a thermal gradient of over 200 degrees. It is a stack of several hundred ring-shaped segments, each a biomineralized disc of alternating pyrite and sphalerite laminae cemented with chitinous cartilage, the whole assembly functioning as a series of thermoelectric cells: the searing base of each ring sits in vent water while its cool upper face bathes in ambient brine, and the dissimilar mineral layers generate a sustained current across the column. There is no mouth, no gut, no head; dissolved ferrous iron and sulfide drawn up through the hollow core are oxidized at internal anodes, the released electrons feeding the column's bioelectricity, while the metal ions are electroplated onto the outer wall as a growing crust of black sulfide scale. A ring of ciliate gills at the segment seams pulses the vent plume through the core, and a weak bioluminescent phosphor — green-blue, excited by the current itself — flickers along the column in slow traveling waves that mark the animal's "breath."

## Behavior

A Galvanostele is born as a single segment nucleated from a drifting plume-bud, anchors to bare fissure rock by electrodepositing a root of magnetite, and then spends its life growing upward toward cooler water, adding a new ring every few weeks at the cool apex while the basal rings slowly cook, mineralize solid, and die into a load-bearing pedestal. It tracks the fissure's shifting heat: if the vent migrates, the column bends its living upper third by selectively powering electrocyte bands on one face, inching over months to re-center on the hottest water. It cannot flee and does not compete; where two columns meet they fuse bases and share current, occasionally grafting into branching chimneys that vent-dwellers use as landmarks. Reproduction is apical: the topmost ring, once it has stored enough electroplated mineral to survive free-floating, pinches off as a buoyant plume-bud carried by the vent's own exhalation to a new fissure, where it sinks, roots, and the gradient begins again.

## Myth

Vent-keepers say a Galvanostele column is a "candle the deep is burning," and read the height of its living apex as a gauge of the fissure's health — a column that has stopped growing is treated as a dead god, its scale pried off for thermoelectric beads that still faintly glow in a closed hand.

## Anatomy

A chain of seven to forty discoidal bladders, each a flattened chitin-composite dome a meter across, linked along a tensegrity notochord of braided collagen. The bladders are not gas-filled but *evacuated* — pumped to near-vacuum by a metalloenzyme sorption pump that, in the same stroke, stores the stripped hydrogen as a solid interstitial hydride in the dome's rim. The hydride is both the pump's sink and the ballast: releasing it back into a bladder floods the chamber with hydrogen, killing the vacuum and dropping buoyancy in a controlled dive. There is no head. A nerve ring per segment coordinates a slow peristalsis; the only gut is a ciliated tube running the notochord's core, fed from a trailing curtain of adhesive mucus filaments fifty meters long.

## Behavior

Aerotholos lives its whole life in the thin air between landmasses and never descends below the canopy's shadow. It orients across the wind-shear gradient — leading dome high, trailing domes low — and streams its mousse net downwind to harvest aeroplankton: drifting spores, stray stratocolumn polyps, the larvae of a hundred other species that ride the updrafts. When the net loads, peristalsis winches it inward along the notochord and the catch is digested in transit, segment by segment. Buoyancy is a conversation with the sky: to climb, the rims re-sorb hydrogen into the hydride, re-evacuating the bladders; to cross a downdraft, they bleed hydrogen back in, descending in a long controlled glide. Reproduction is fission — a chain pinches at a weak joint during strong shear, and each half regrows the missing domes over a season, the new bladders seeded from a hydride-rich bud on the notochord.

## Myth

Skyfarers crossing the open Aether watch for the slow geometry of an Aerotholos at dusk and read its chain like a wind-flown prayer flag. A chain that holds all forty domes without pinching, they say, is carrying a soul that has not yet chosen a landmass to be born on — and to break its net, even accidentally, is to delay that birth by a lifetime.
